Medication Errors

Medication errors refer to mistakes that occur during the prescribing, dispensing, or administering of medication. These errors can include prescribing the wrong medication or dose, dispensing the wrong medication, or administering medication incorrectly. Medication errors can have serious consequences, including injury or death. To prevent medication errors, healthcare providers should use clear and accurate prescribing practices, double-check medications before administering them, and use technology such as barcode scanning to ensure that the right medication is being given to the right patient.

Patient Safety

Patient safety is a major focus of healthcare organizations and regulatory agencies, with efforts aimed at identifying and reducing risks, promoting transparency and accountability, and encouraging a culture of safety within healthcare organizations.

Patient safety refers to the prevention of errors and adverse events in healthcare delivery. This includes preventing harm to patients from the care that is intended to help them, and it encompasses a wide range of issues related to the quality and safety of healthcare.
